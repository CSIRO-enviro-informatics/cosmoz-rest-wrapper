'Dose Site' is the neutron monitor station from NMDB.eu with the nearest Cut-Off Rigidity and reliable data 'Lattice Water' is the amount of water trapped in the mineral structures of the soil it is defined as the amount of water released at 1000_C preceded by drying at 105_C. Analysis is undertaken ACTLABS Canada. See Hawdon et al. 2014 for details. 'Soil Organic matter' is the amount of organic matter in the soil expressed as a water equivalent. Analysis is undertaken ACTLABS Canada. See Hawdon et al. 2014 for details. 'Average Bulk Density' is calculated from field calibrations at each site 'Cut-Off Rigidity' is calculated from Latitude and longitude using this calculator http://cosmos.hwr.arizona.edu/Util/rigidity.php 'Average N0' is the counting rate for completely dry soil defined using equation A1 of Desilets et al. (2010) in form presented by Hawdon et al. (2014) in equation 7. 'Reference Pressure' is the atmospheric pressure at the given site altitude according to the Atmospheric Standards Formula (U.S. Standard Atmosphere, 1976) 'Intensity References Value' is the neutron monitor count for the Dose Site on the 1 May 2011 'Vapor Reference Value' is the water content of completely dry air, i.e. 0 'Atmospheric Depth' is the reference pressure divided by gravity 'Atmospheric Attenuation Coefficient' is calculated from Desilets and Zreda (2003) equation 11 'Sea Level Reference Pressure' is the atmospheric pressure at sea level according to the Atmospheric Standards Formula (U.S. Standard Atmosphere, 1976) 'Sea Level Atmospheric depth' is the reference pressure at sea level divided by gravity 'Sea Level Atmospheric attenuation coefficient' is calculated from Desilets and Zreda (2003) equation 11 'Elevation Scale' and 'Latitude Scale' are calculated following Desilets and Zreda (2006) and allow correction of site count values to a common reference point (in this case sea level at the equator) so that they can be directly compared.
